# Web Game Portal Comparison (2025–2026)

Honest comparison of the major distribution paths for web games. All figures dated and sourced; treat platform-specific terms as [LIKELY] subject to change — verify directly before signing.

## At-a-glance

| Portal | MAU (2025) | Strength | Weakness | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Poki** | ~100M (June 2025) | Highest reach + revenue per featured slot; full-service partner support | Curated (~1,000 active games); web-exclusive required | Polished, production-ready games; partnership-grade studios |
| **CrazyGames** | ~30M | Open submission; +50% rev for 2-month exclusivity option | Less curation = more competition for traffic | Indies who can't get Poki to take a meeting |
| **Y8** | Lower; legacy traffic | Easy submission, large library | Older audience, lower payouts | Volume play, aggregator strategy |
| **Newgrounds** | Niche / cult | Animator-focused community; high engagement when match | Limited reach for non-Newgrounds-aesthetic games | Pixel art, Flash-aesthetic, animated games |
| **itch.io (HTML5)** | Indie audience | No exclusivity; full control; community | No traffic generation; payment processor risk (2025) | Prototype/feedback platform; jam games |
| **Aggregators** (Game Distribution, FRVR) | Variable | Multi-portal distribution from one upload | Lower per-portal payouts; less curation | Volume games, no time for individual partnerships |

## Detailed profiles

### Poki

**Status (Dec 2025):** ~100M MAU; 1B plays/month; won Dutch Game Awards Best in Business 2025. ~1,000 games at any time, ~600 active studios. Top studio revenues grew 10× over five years; some now earn up to ~€1M/year.

**Model:** Partnership-driven. Poki provides QA, UI consulting, marketing, monetization. Curated — they choose what to feature. Submissions accepted but acceptance rate is low.

**Revenue:** Ad-supported. Standard split is rev-share — exact terms confidential per partner. **Critical kicker:** when players come from outside Poki (bookmarks, search, your social), the developer gets **100% of revenue**. This is structural arbitrage — see `direct-traffic-arbitrage.md`.

**Exclusivity:** Web exclusivity required (you can't put the game on CrazyGames or competitor portals). Steam/mobile parallel allowed.

**Tech requirements:** ~2 second TTI on mid-range mobile; bundle ≤2 MB initial. See `bundle-budget-strategy.md`.

**How to get in:** High-quality demo, Poki contact form. Be persistent but not annoying. Demonstrate the game has built-in audience appeal (early traction, viral moments). Indie history doesn't matter as much as game quality + audience fit.

### CrazyGames

**Status:** ~30M MAU; 300M+ plays/month; 1,000+ developers.

**Model:** Open submission. Less curation. Pure volume + algorithmic surfacing.

**Revenue:** Rev-share, exact percentages confidential. **2-month exclusivity option grants +50% revenue share** — significant if you can absorb the lock-in.

**Exclusivity:** Optional; rewarded with higher rev share. Standard non-exclusive distribution allowed.

**Tech requirements:** Same as Poki essentially — fast load, mobile-friendly, no required login.

**Gameplay-conversion benchmark:** ~73% target for top games (≥1 minute play per Play Now click). See `gameplay-conversion-ratio.md`.

**How to get in:** Submit through their developer portal. Less gatekeeping than Poki. Approval typically within weeks.

### Y8

**Status:** Lower MAU than Poki/CrazyGames; legacy player base.

**Model:** Aggregation + own audience. Older audience demographic.

**Revenue:** Rev-share with lower payouts than Poki/CrazyGames per impression.

**Best for:** Volume strategy. Submit alongside major portals to capture residual traffic.

### Newgrounds

**Status:** Niche but engaged community. Strong for animation-adjacent or pixel-art games.

**Model:** Community-first. Players are creators too. Comments and engagement matter more than raw traffic.

**Revenue:** Limited direct monetization; lower payouts. Value is community + audience-building, not direct revenue.

**Best for:** Specific aesthetics — pixel art, Flash-era, retro, cult — that resonate with the Newgrounds community. Don't post a generic puzzle game here and expect traction.

### itch.io (HTML5)

**Status (April 2026):** Recovering from mid-2025 disruptions. NSFW content deindex (July 2025) and Stripe payment-processor restrictions (Sept 2025) caused 100+ day payout delays for some sellers. Payouts gradually resumed late 2025 with new compliance terms.

**Model:** No curation, full developer control. No exclusivity. Free uploads. You set price (or "name your price").

**Revenue:** Player-paid (donations, set prices). itch.io takes a configurable cut (default 10%, dev can override). **Don't depend on itch.io as primary processor** — diversify.

**Best for:** Prototype/feedback platform. Game jams (itch is the canonical jam host). Building early community before portal release. Demo distribution.

**Don't use for:** Sole revenue channel for serious commercial release.

### Aggregators (Game Distribution, FRVR, Spil)

**Status:** Multi-portal distribution from one upload + SDK integration.

**Model:** You implement their SDK; they distribute to dozens of portals automatically.

**Revenue:** Per-portal rates lower than direct partnership; aggregator takes their cut on top.

**Best for:** Volume strategy when you don't have time for individual portal relationships. Or as supplemental distribution alongside Poki/CrazyGames.

## Distribution strategy patterns

### "Polished single-game" path
1. Build for Poki spec (≤2MB, fast load, mobile-first)
2. Demo + pitch Poki partnership
3. If accepted: full-service partnership, web-exclusive, focus on quality
4. If declined: CrazyGames 2-month exclusive (+50% rev share), then non-exclusive expansion

### "Indie launch" path
1. itch.io for prototype + jam audience
2. Iterate based on feedback
3. Steam Page + wishlist campaign
4. Steam launch + parallel CrazyGames release
5. After traction: pitch Poki

### "Volume" path
1. Build multiple small games for aggregator SDK (Game Distribution, FRVR)
2. Auto-distribute to dozens of portals
3. Optimize the ones that get traction
4. Migrate winners to direct Poki/CrazyGames partnerships

## Honest cautions

- **Poki acceptance is competitive.** Don't build a strategy that requires Poki acceptance to break even. Build a game that earns even on second-tier portals.
- **Web-exclusivity is a serious commitment.** If you go Poki-exclusive, you cannot release on CrazyGames or any other portal. Steam and mobile remain available.
- **TikTok organic reach for indie games collapsed in 2025.** Don't plan marketing around viral TikTok devlogs — same content that hit 800K views in 2024 now averages 300–800. Verify with current data before investing.
- **itch.io payment processor risk** (mid-2025) — diversify; don't trust single-platform payout.
- **Web3/blockchain games** — toxic association with the casual web-game audience as of 2026; Poki/CrazyGames don't promote them; no resurgence in indie discourse.
