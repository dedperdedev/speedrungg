# Schell's Lenses — Curated Subset

From Jesse Schell, "The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses." 100+ lenses total. This is a curated subset — the lenses that fire most often during web-game design reviews.

Use during `/design-review`. Pick 3–5 lenses relevant to the current question. Don't apply all 100.

## L4: Lens of Surprise
- Where does my game surprise the player?
- Are there enough surprises in the first session?
- Are surprises preserved (not spoiled by trailers, tutorials, achievement names)?

## L7: Lens of Endogenous Value
- What in this game does the player care about that exists ONLY in this game?
- Why do they care?
- How can we make them care more?

## L8: Lens of Problem Solving
- What problem(s) is the player solving?
- Are the problems satisfying to solve?
- Does the player encounter the same problem repeatedly without growth?

## L14: Lens of the Player
- What does my player like?
- What do they want from a game session?
- Is my game serving them or me?

## L20: Lens of Skill vs. Chance
- Where in this game does the player apply skill?
- Where does chance dominate?
- Is the ratio right for this audience?

## L26: Lens of Goals
- Are the goals clear?
- Are short-term, mid-term, long-term goals all present?
- Does the player generate their own goals?

## L33: Lens of Meaningful Choices
- What choices is the player making?
- Are choices meaningful (different paths, different consequences)?
- Are there too many trivial choices (decision fatigue)?
- Are there too few (no agency)?

## L35: Lens of Reward
- What rewards does the game offer?
- Are rewards aligned with what the player values?
- Is there reward variability (mix of expected + surprise)?

## L46: Lens of Punishment
- What punishments does the game inflict?
- Do they teach, or just frustrate?
- Is the punishment proportional to the offense?

## L53: Lens of Emotion
- What emotion is the player feeling, moment to moment?
- What emotions do we want them to feel that they aren't?
- What emotions are they feeling that we don't want?

## L72: Lens of Curiosity
- What questions does my game put in the player's head?
- Are those questions answered satisfyingly?
- Are new questions raised as old ones close?

## L86: Lens of Beauty
- Is my game beautiful in its own way?
- What kind of beauty (visual, mechanical, emotional)?
- Does the beauty serve the game or distract from it?

## L93: Lens of Community
- What does my community do together?
- Does the game support community emergence (sharing, modding, social)?
- Does the community amplify or dilute the experience?

## How to use

In a review session, pick 3–5 lenses. For each, write a one-paragraph honest answer. If the answer is "I don't know," that's the most valuable finding.

## Citation format

When citing in design docs:

> Through L33 (Meaningful Choices): the player has 4 weapons but they're DPS-equivalent — the choice is cosmetic, not meaningful.
