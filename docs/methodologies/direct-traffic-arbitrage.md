# Direct Traffic Arbitrage

A structural opportunity often missed by indie developers on Poki specifically. **Players who arrive at your Poki page from outside Poki (bookmarks, search, social, your own site) generate 100% of revenue for the developer.**

This is not a bug; it's Poki's developer-incentive policy. If you're already going Poki-exclusive (or planning to), driving direct traffic to your Poki URL converts every off-platform marketing dollar into 100%-rev arbitrage instead of standard rev-share.

## The math

Let's say standard rev share gives developer X% of ad revenue per play.

```
Player from Poki carousel:    Developer earns X% × revenue
Player from your tweet:        Developer earns 100% × revenue
```

If X is 50%, every off-platform player is **2× as valuable** as a portal-driven one. If X is 30%, off-platform is **3.3× as valuable**.

This is rare in distribution economics. Most platforms (Steam, App Store) take their cut regardless of who brought the player.

## How to exploit (without breaking Poki's terms)

### 1. Own a landing page
Build a simple site for the game (game-name.com or a section of your dev site). Link directly to your Poki URL. Use your landing page to:
- Answer "what is this game" before the player clicks
- Provide context (devlog, screenshots, story)
- Drive curiosity that the in-game can't easily do
- **CTA: "Play now on Poki"** linking to your Poki URL

### 2. Social presence drives direct traffic
- Twitter/X devlog posts with Poki link (not just "the game" — specific URL)
- YouTube devlogs ending with "play now: [Poki URL]"
- Reddit posts in relevant subreddits with link
- Discord community where you announce updates

### 3. Email list
Even small (100-1000 subscribers). Each launch / update email with Poki link drives high-conversion traffic.

### 4. Press / coverage
Indie blogs, gaming press, YouTubers. Each piece of coverage that links your Poki URL is direct traffic.

### 5. SEO
Optimize your landing page + Poki page name for search. "[game name] online" or "[game name] free" terms. Long tail counts.

### 6. Cross-promotion
If you have other games (or partner with other devs), cross-link. Player finishing Game A gets a "what to play next" pointing to Game B's Poki URL.

## What NOT to do

- **Don't game Poki's own traffic** by, say, having your game embed code that bounces players back through external links to inflate "off-platform" attribution. This violates partnership terms and gets you delisted.
- **Don't promise things to off-platform players you can't deliver** ("exclusive content if you come from my site" — Poki's hands are tied here).
- **Don't underestimate Poki's own discovery** — their carousel still drives the bulk of plays. Direct traffic is supplement, not replacement.
- **Don't make a "second game site" that competes with Poki's listing** — Poki wants players to engage with their ecosystem (recommendations, related games). Your landing page should *route* to Poki, not replicate it.

## When this matters most

| Game stage | Direct traffic value |
|---|---|
| Pre-launch | Very high — pre-build audience for launch day spike |
| Launch week | High — every direct visitor counts toward 100% rev plus early portal-algorithm signal |
| Steady state | Medium — supplement Poki's own traffic |
| Long tail | High again — Poki traffic decays but direct can be evergreen |

## Operational checklist

For a Poki-distributed game:

- [ ] Own a landing page (even single-page is fine)
- [ ] Game's Poki URL is canonical CTA on the landing page
- [ ] Devlog / social presence linking to Poki URL
- [ ] Press kit available with Poki URL prominent
- [ ] Email list (free Mailchimp/Buttondown) for major updates
- [ ] Each social post about the game includes the Poki URL
- [ ] Track which referrers drive most plays (Poki provides referrer breakdown)
- [ ] Double down on highest-performing direct sources

## The cousin pattern: CrazyGames + own site

CrazyGames doesn't have an explicit 100%-off-platform rule (verify their current terms before assuming). But the same principle applies in spirit: own a presence outside the portal, drive traffic, build audience that's portable across portals if you ever leave.
